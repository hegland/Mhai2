# Access Strategies for Academic Papers

When bulk-downloading papers, always provide users with **tiered access methods** in case some papers are paywalled or require institutional access.

---

## Tier 1: Free & Immediate Access

### arXiv
- **Status:** ✅ Most papers available, 100% free
- **URL pattern:** `https://arxiv.org/abs/{arxiv_id}` (abstract) or `https://arxiv.org/pdf/{arxiv_id}` (PDF)
- **Quality:** Scientifically identical to published versions (preprints are official author submissions)
- **Pros:** No paywall, fast, reliable, citation-ready
- **Cons:** Formatting may differ slightly from published journal version
- **When to use:** Default first choice for all author searches

**Key insight:** arXiv preprints are NOT drafts — they are official peer-reviewed manuscripts. Scientifically identical to published versions. Only typesetting/formatting differs (journal logos, page numbers, some figure reorganization). Fully citable and widely accepted in academia.

### Alternative Open Archives
- **INSPIRE-HEP** (physics/math): https://inspirehep.net/
- **Zenodo** (EU-funded research): https://zenodo.org/
- **DOAJ** (Directory of Open Access Journals): https://doaj.org/

---

## Tier 2: Institutional Access

### University Library Subscriptions (e.g., ANU)
- **Access:** Log in with institutional email via proxy or direct sign-in
- **Common journals:** Nature Reviews Physics, Journal of Chemical Physics, Physical Review (APS suite), Soft Matter (RSC)
- **Method:**
  1. Find DOI of paper (usually on journal or paper metadata)
  2. Go to journal website directly
  3. Click "Institutional login" or "Log in with your institution"
  4. Select university from list
  5. Sign in with institutional credentials
  6. Download PDF
- **Pros:** Direct access to official versions, institutional guarantee
- **Cons:** Only works if university has subscription; may be slower than arXiv

**For ANU faculty:** Use https://library.anu.edu.au/ search, then access via institutional proxy.

### Interlibrary Loan (ILL)
- **When:** Paper not in institution's collection
- **Process:** Request through library; usually arrives in 5–7 business days
- **Cost:** Often free for university staff
- **Pros:** Reaches papers not in subscription list; permanent copy
- **Cons:** Slow; requires submission + approval

## Tier 3: Direct Author Contact

### Email the Author
- **Effectiveness:** ~90% positive response rate among academics
- **Response time:** Usually 24–48 hours
- **How to write:**
  ```
  Subject: Request for preprint of [paper title]
  
  Dear Prof. [Author],
  
  I'm [Your Name], a [position] at [Institution] working on [brief research focus].
  I'd like to read your paper "[Title]" (arXiv:XXXX.XXXXX / [DOI]) and would 
  appreciate a preprint if you're able to share.
  
  Thanks,
  [Name]
  [Email]
  [Affiliation]
  ```
- **Pros:** Author often grateful for interest; may provide preprint + related papers
- **Cons:** Requires waiting; depends on author responsiveness

**Tip:** Include your affiliation and research context. Most academics are happy to share their work with interested researchers.

### Collaborators & Network
- **Ask co-authors on your project** — they may have copies from collaborations
- **Post in research communities** (Reddit r/AcademicPapers, ResearchGate) — but verify legality in your region

---

## Decision Tree

**When you find a paper:**

1. **Check arXiv first** — If available (most math/physics papers are), download immediately. Done.

2. **If NOT on arXiv:**
   - Is it paywalled (journal only)? → Try institutional proxy (Tier 2)
   - Still blocked? → Email author (Tier 3)

3. **If arXiv version available BUT you want official journal version:**
   - For reading/research? → Use arXiv (same science, no paywall)
   - For formal citation/publication? → Try institutional proxy; otherwise preprint is fine

**Rule of thumb:** Never wait for a paywalled journal version if the arXiv preprint is available. You have everything you need scientifically.

---

## Region-Specific Legal Notes

- **US/EU:** Sharing preprints is legal and standard practice. Authors often maintain a personal copy online (check their institutional page).
- **China:** Similar norms to EU/US; arXiv widely used.
- **Some countries:** Check copyright rules before requesting PDFs outside arXiv. When in doubt, use arXiv or email author.

## Key Lesson

**Always inform users that arXiv preprints are scientifically complete and fully citable.** This eliminates false urgency around paywalled versions and lets them start reading immediately. Pair bulk downloads with this context so they don't sit in a "need to access journal version" state.
